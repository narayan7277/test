Couples therapy techniques at Mpower



\r\n\r\n1. Listening with intention

\r\n\r\n\"Reflective listening,\" explains Laura Louis, a licensed psychologist at Atlanta Couple Therapy, \"is a tremendously useful exercise where the couple takes turns being active listeners.\"

\r\n\r\nInstead of using \"you\" sentences, use \"I\" words. Instead of saying \"You're bad for doing X,\" say \"I feel hurt when you do X.\"

\r\n\r\n\"When partners take turns being active listeners, it improves the couple's communication and conflict resolution skills,\" Louis explains.\r\n\r\n

2. Psychotherapy with a strong emotional focus

\r\n\r\nEmotionally focused therapy is a technique utilized by many therapists (EFT).

\r\n\r\nAccording to Ansley Campbell, a clinical director at The Summit Wellness Group, the goal is for couples to \"identify maladaptive patterns within the relationship that are interfering with safe ties and attachments.\"

\r\n\r\nShe explains that people \"learn and use strategies to repair or develop safe and secure attachments inside the partnership.\"\r\n\r\n

3. The use of narrative therapy

\r\n\r\nNarrative therapy involves people recreating their stories and articulating their difficulties in a narrative format. This can assist them in realizing that no single story can possibly capture the entirety of their experience.

\r\n\r\n\"Inconsistencies and contradictions will always exist,\" says Sam Nabil, CEO and main therapist at Naya Clinics.

\r\n\r\nCouples who believe their relationship is failing because of both of their flaws may benefit from narrative therapy.

\r\n\r\n\"These couples often believe they're in this love quagmire and emotional misery because they've always been a 'failure,' and it's what they 'deserve,\" adds Nabil.\r\n\r\n

4. The Gottman Technique

\r\n\r\nThe Gottman Method is a well-known technique used by couples’ therapists. The method is intended to assist couples in deepening their understanding of one another while also resolving problems in their relationship.

\r\n\r\nThe Gottman Institute has been conducting research for almost 40 years. The Gottman Institute offers live workshops and take-home training materials for couples, but many therapists have also been trained using its methods.

\r\n\r\n\r\n5. Relationship treatment with Imago

\r\n\r\nDr. Harville Hendrix and Dr. Helen LaKelly Hunt established Imago relationship therapy in 1980, which emphasizes the link between adult relationships and childhood experiences.

\r\n\r\nThe therapy aims to make couples more empathic and understanding of one another by addressing early trauma.

\r\n\r\n6. Problem-solving treatment

\r\n\r\nSolution-focused therapy is a paradigm to explore if you're dealing with a specific problem or want to work toward a specific goal.

\r\n\r\n\"A short-term goal-focused evidence-based therapeutic method that helps clients change by developing solutions rather than wallowing,\" according to the Institute for Solution-Focused Therapy.
